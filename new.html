Coffee grains are the best snack ever made

I know, I know—this might sound like an unpopular opinion, but hear me out. The idea of munching on coffee grains as a snack might raise a few eyebrows, but it's a notion worth exploring. Curiosity has always been a driving force for humanity, leading us to discover new tastes and experiences. It was this same curiosity that led me to pop a coffee grain into my mouth for the first time. The result? A delightful revelation that I believe deserves more attention.

Brazil and Coffee (Every family has its own Rory and Lorelai)

Living in Brazil, coffee isn't just a beverage; it's a cultural staple. As the world's largest producer of coffee, Brazil contributes approximately one-third of global coffee production. This deep-rooted connection to coffee permeates our daily lives, much like the Gilmore Girls' infamous coffee addiction. In many Brazilian households, it's not uncommon to enjoy a cup of coffee late into the evening. Over time, this habitual consumption has desensitized many of us to its stimulating effects, making coffee a comforting constant rather than just a morning pick-me-up.

The diet bitter overexpensive snack of the week

Now, let's talk about coffee grains as a snack. At first glance, it might seem unconventional, but consider this: coffee beans are low in calories and packed with antioxidants. A 50-gram serving contains about 260 calories. That means each individual coffee bean has roughly 5.2 calories.

Comparatively, many low-calorie snacks on the market are both expensive and less satisfying. Imagine purchasing a trendy health snack that costs a small fortune and leaves you hungry an hour later. Now, consider a serving of 30 coffee grains. At approximately 5.2 calories per bean, that's a mere 156 calories. If we estimate the cost of coffee beans at $0.12 per gram, and with each bean weighing about 1 gram, a 30-bean snack would cost around $3.60.

Sure, it's a bit on the pricey side for a snack, but isn't that the hallmark of the latest fitness trends? Overpriced, slightly bitter, and touted by influencers as the next big thing. So, here's my (sarcastic) prediction: soon, we'll see fitness enthusiasts swapping their kale chips for artisanal, hand-roasted coffee bean snacks. After all, nothing says "health-conscious" like chewing on the source of your morning brew.

In conclusion, while it might not be for everyone, snacking on coffee grains offers a unique combination of flavor, cultural homage, and a nod to the ever-evolving landscape of health trends. Give it a try—you might just find yourself reaching for a handful of beans instead of that energy bar.
